## General

The new letter deals with the e-invoicing obligation for transactions between entrepreneurs resident in Germany. This obligation has been considered a passive obligation (receipt of invoices) since 2025, but is not to be introduced as an active obligation until 2027 or 2028.

The new letter is essentially intended to transfer the letter of 2024 to the VAT Circular, but without repealing this letter, which instead amends and supplements it in some points.

## Changes to the 2024 Letter

The letter provides a comprehensive overview of the errors identified in the e-invoice. Firstly, it differentiates between format errors and format-specific business rule errors:

* **Format errors:** Invoice files which do not comply with the permitted syntaxes or their technical specifications may contain format errors.
* **Business rules** are technical regulations which govern the logical dependencies of the information contained in an e-invoice.

According to the BMF, both categories of errors can be checked with a suitable validation application.

## Key Takeaways

* Format errors can result in invoices not meeting e-invoice requirements.
* Errors in business rules can lead to an e-invoice being present but not in order.
* Validation applications can help identify both types of errors.
* The introduction of e-invoicing is of utmost importance for German economic operators.
