**Key Information on the SME Portal**

**1. The New EU SME Scheme** 

Since January 2025, EU-based small businesses can apply for VAT exemptions not only in their home country but also in other EU member states, provided they remain below the relevant turnover thresholds.

**2. Portal Features and Tools** 

The EU Commission’s new information hub currently offers:

* **SME-on-the-web**: A digital tool to verify small business identification numbers.
* **Comprehensive Guidelines**: Detailed documents and guides explaining the requirements and consequences of the new regime (available in multiple languages, including German).
* **Regulatory Overviews**: Presentations of small business regulations across different member states.

**3. Current Limitations** 

The portal is still a work in progress:

* **Missing Features**: A "simulator" tool to help businesses assess their eligibility is not yet available.
* **Incomplete Data**: Information regarding specific regulations in some member states is still being finalized.
* **Language**: Much of the core website content is currently only available in English or via machine translation.

**4. Legal Note** 

The guidelines and tools provided on the portal are informative but not legally binding. They serve as a resource and do not replace professional tax advice.
