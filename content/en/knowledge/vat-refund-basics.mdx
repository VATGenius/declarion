**Core Principles of the VAT Refund Procedure**

**1. Eligibility and Substantive Requirements**

* Non-Resident Status: The applicant must not be resident in the country of refund or carry out transactions that require local VAT registration.
* Business Purpose: VAT must have been incurred for legitimate business activities.
* Local Restrictions: Refund eligibility follows the laws of the country of refund. Some nations exclude specific costs like entertainment, luxury goods, or certain travel expenses from deduction.

**2. Refunds within the European Union (EU)**

* Electronic Portal: Entrepreneurs apply via the online portal provided by their home country's tax authority.
* Verification: The home tax office confirms the applicant’s status as a taxable person and forwards the request to the Member State of refund.
* Deadlines: Applications must be submitted by September 30 of the year following the invoice date.
* Minimum Thresholds: Generally €400 for quarterly applications or €50 for an annual period.
* Digital Evidence: Scanned copies of invoices are often required for amounts exceeding €1,000 (fuel €250).

**3. Refunds from Non-EU (Third) Countries**

* Reciprocity: A refund is only possible if the home country and the refunding country have a mutual agreement (reciprocity).
* Direct Application: Applications must usually be sent directly to the foreign tax authority using their specific forms and language.
* Documentation: Most third countries require original paper invoices and an official Certificate of Taxable Status (issued by the home tax office) proving the applicant is a registered entrepreneur.
* Processing Time: Authorities typically aim to decide within six months, though payment times vary by jurisdiction.

**4. Key Deadlines and Interest**

* Missing the application deadline (especially the EU's September 30th cutoff) usually results in a total loss of the refund.
* In the EU, if an authority exceeds the legal processing time (usually 4 months and 10 days), the applicant may be entitled to interest on the refund amount.
